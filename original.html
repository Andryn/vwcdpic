VWCDPIC

VWCDPIC Audio Interface Adapter (US$49.00)
Allows you to use most audio devices (such as MP3 players) with your stock head unit in 1998+ Volkswagen automobiles.

The Volkswagen 1998 and later automobiles are equipped with a trunk mounted CD Changer interface. This is great for hooking up an extra audio device to your car's sound system because it is pre-wired for you. However, Volkswagen's OEM head unit locks this interface from being used by anything but a Volkswagen CD Changer by muting the audio inputs unless there is a valid data stream coming from the CD Changer. Of course, this data stream is not documented by Volkswagen.

The VWCDPIC is a "lock pick," if you will, that unlocks the CD Changer interface by sending the required data stream, fooling the head unit into thinking a real Volkswagen CD Changer is connected. With a VWCDPIC, you'll be able to connect any audio device up to your stock OEM head unit. If you are connecting a PJRC MP3 Player, you'll even be able to remotely control your player from the head unit's buttons up front (after you build a cable described here).



  • Provides standard pre-amp RCA input jacks for easily connecting your own audio device (such as an MP3 player). Direct auxiliary input, not FM modulated!

  • Fools the stock head unit into thinking a VW CD Changer is connected, allowing you to listen to your audio device through your vehicle's built-in speakers when you select "CD" at the head unit.

  • Can be used to remote control a PJRC MP3 Player or PC using serial data commands. This requires an extra cable that is not included, but you can make one easily from information on the VWCDPIC Pinouts.

  • Remote control Pocket PC PDAs when using the third party shareware player RadioPlayer.

  • Remote controls the Archos Jukebox MP3 players with Rockbox open source firmware. Again, you need a special cable that is not included but can be made easily.

  • Remote control Apple iPod 3rd generation and later MP3 players using Z-Man's new iPod-VW firmware (available in the Developer's Area).

    Caveats

    • 2003+ New Beetles with the CD Changer plug in the center arm rest console are difficult to install into. You may be in for some hand crunching. Gwagg has posted some pictures at NewBeetle.org that show how he installed his VWCDPIC in the center arm rest console.
    • Low voltage audio players may not produce enough volume to work well with your head unit. See the FAQ for more.
    • Some power systems for powering PCs from the car will cause the VWCDPIC to operate unreliably (inverters especially). You may need to experiment with different power supplies to get it working well. Click here for a diagram that should help explain the problem.
    • A ground loop isolator may be needed to solve electrical noise issues if you power your audio player from the car's power system. These can be purchased from your local electronics store (RadioShack, etc).
